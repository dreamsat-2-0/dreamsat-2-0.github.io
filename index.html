DreamSat 2.0: Towards a General Single-View Asteroid 3D Reconstruction

Authors

Santiago Diaz1,4, Xinghui Hu2,4, Josiane Uwumukiza3,4, Giovanni Lavezzi2, Victor Rodriguez-Fernandez1, Richard Linares2

1 Universidad Politécnica de Madrid   2 Massachusetts Institute of Technology   3 Wellesley College   4 Equal contribution

Paper Code DreamSat 1.0

Abstract

3D reconstruction offers significant potential for enhancing asteroid exploration and autonomous spacecraft navigation. In this work, we introduce DreamSat-2.0, a pipeline integrating three state-of-the-art models—Hunyuan-3D, Trellis-3D, and Ouroboros-3D—and conduct a comparative analysis on custom spacecraft and asteroid datasets using a suite of 2D and 3D metrics. New benchmarks are established over previous DreamSat framework, which demonstrated a relative gain (+2.53\% PSNR). This study sets a new performance standard, with Hunyuan-3D achieving top scores (20.93 PSNR, 0.9291 SSIM, 0.1124 LPIPS) on spacecraft but its best geometric accuracy on asteroids (0.5619 IoU). This reveals that performance is domain-dependent.
